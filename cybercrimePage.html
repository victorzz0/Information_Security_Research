Cybercrime

Introduction

  • Cybercrime is an illegal practice that uses networks (eg. the Internet) and computers to cause damage to victims.

Here is a video to explain cybercrime:

  • The cybercrime research group at UCL “aims to better understand the economic, social, and engineering aspects of cybercriminal operations.”1
  • Research in this area is important as it helps improve methods to disrupt illegal practices and to design secure systems.

Research

The cybercrime group at UCL studies many acts of cybercrime. They are presently researching botnets, specifically the ones "that are used to send email spam or to generate virtual currencies."5

The group is also looking at the "anonymity guarantees of Bitcoin"6. Bitcoin is an application of cryptocurrency (cryptography is used to control and secure transactions.) Check out this link for further information on Bitcoin: What is Bitcoin?
Moreover, the researchers analyse how cybercriminals exploit social networks online in order to develop methods to prevent such practices.
Dr Gianluca Stringhini who is the cybercrime group lead at UCL has been researching online dating scams and money laundering schemes.

Impact

The results of research in cybercrime are used to gain improved understanding of cybercrime practices and to develop preventive techniques. Researchers in the UCL group work with other research groups and companies such as "Microsoft Research, IBM Research, Yahoo!, Twitter, and Symantec"7, which allows their research to positively impact the real world.
