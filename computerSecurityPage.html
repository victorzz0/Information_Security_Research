Computer Security

Introduction

Computer security is essentially the protection of hardware, software and stored data in a computer system from possible damages, theft and misuse.

Research

The researchers at UCL study many aspects of computer security. Listed below are some of the researches done recently:

One of them is about the security of cryptographic hardware system. A cryptographic hardware system is essentially a system that utilizes many individual hardware components to perform encryption to confidential data and information. Sometimes, hackers can compromise the whole system by only attacking certain vulnerable components, thus resulting in a theft or misuse of data encrypted. This is really really bad: imagine your card numbers being stolen and used to purchase expensive things without your permission! In a research paper published recently, Dr. Danezis was part of the research team that pioneered a way to build a more secure system where the system will only be compromised when ALL of the individual components are compromised!(1) You can find out more about the research here!

Another one of them is about building more secure hardware systems based on the “principle of least privilege”. Below is a short Youtube video explaining what the “principle of least privilege” is:

In a research paper published last year, Dr. Murdoch was part of a research team that found a way to allow both the hardware and software of a computer system to make better use of the “principle of least privilege”! This is achieved through improving the current technique of breaking software into smaller isolated pieces and improving the access control of the underlying hardware.(2) You can find out more about the research here!

I am sure many of you have heard of and used the Android system in your phones. In that case, you must have encountered this scene of permission authentication in your phone:

Dr. Stringhini and Professor Sasse were part of a group that analyzed the new permission model in the Android system. They found that in general the new model is well received by users and users are persistent to allow access to resources that are directly related to their main functionality (e.g. Camera for Instagram). In addition, the study also showed that users’ behavior is consistent regarding the resources they allow to social media applications to access.(3) You can find out more about the research here!

Impact

The researches done have immense potential to make the electronic devices that we use daily more secure. By enhancing encryption and access control, they make our personal data less vulnerable to theft by unlawful attackers and malicious software. Now you can have much more confidence saving things like your credit card information in applications such as Google Play or Amazon and not worrying about them being stolen.
