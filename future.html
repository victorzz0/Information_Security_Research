Future Directions

Here is an interview with Dr. George Danezis about the possible future developments in information security:

Generally, the current information security technology is based on the network security technology, which is an important direction for future development. We believe many well-defined theories will be applied into practice finally, such as zero-knowledge protocols proposed by Professor Jens Groth at UCL. In addition, it is noted that the common development between different disciplines such as computer security and artificial intelligence would make a huge difference to our world. It was hard to imagine that a program could ever beat the No.1 Go professional player in the world. It did. This is AlphaGo, developed by Dr Demis Hassabis and his colleagues whom graduated with doctor degree from UCL. To conclude, there are several factors that will drive security research, such as artificial intelligence, crowdsourcing, blockchain technologies and so on. Here is a photo showing five possivle directions and open issues.

Our world has been experiencing a complete technological revolution that is changing, reshaping and redefining every aspect in our lives1, while the perceived need of computer users and communication privacy has brought information security out of the shadow world of espionage and secrecy with which it has been linked traditionally. Therefore, more advanced information security technology became particularly important in such an age of the Internet.
